Second Stone Legend of the Hidden World

As a core gameplay programmer, I developed and maintained gameplay systems from the ground up, working closely with design and art to bring features to life. My contributions spanned player abilities, enemy AI, boss encounters, NPC behavior, UI/UX, SFX/VFX integration, and material pipelines. I was involved in both the technical implementation and the design process, helping shape the moment-to-moment gameplay, overall feel, and flow of the game.

Examples of areas I have developed

Zaraxas - Boss Fight

I was solely responsible for implementing the logic/balancing for the boss fight. This fight is longer into the game so we wanted it to feel quite intense.

All the visual effects you see in the fight were created by me — explosions, tornado, the particles on the sword i'm especially proud of.

Manastone fusing system

Here is a demonstration of a Manastone system I implemented. This system allows players to fuse existing manastones together to create new elements with new abilities!

Here you can see the player collecting their fused manastones. I didn't want UI for this part so the players could collect the manastones with ease and continue adventuring right away!

Kugutsu - Boss Fight

A showcase of an intro cinematic I created inside Unreal Engine for a boss fight in the game.

I was responsible for creating the level, gameplay logic and visual effects for this boss fight.

Because the game is a RPG & Platformer hybrid, I wanted to add a platforming section to this boss fight. This area and the effects composition is entirely my work.

The two separate areas in this fight is in the same level which allows instantenous transitions between them. We just move the player with a fast fade out/in.

Haruki - Boss Fight

I was responsible for the level, VFX and gameplay logic for this boss fight as well. The level is a bit darker than it actually is in the actual game. The darker arena was intended to better show off the bosses attacks and light up the arena with the attacks.

In this fight i wanted to also add a platforming element. And the arena shifts and changes based on how much the boss attacks forcing the player to platform to not fall off the arena. It's worth to mention that the player has a rewind mechanic in game which automatically triggers when falling down into the pit taking a portion of the players health. So it's not game over instantly if the player falls off.

Final Thoughts

Working on Second Stone was an incredible journey of growth and creativity. I like to showcase boss fights in particular because they are a culmination of many different systems working together and require a lot of iteration to get right. Other systems that I'm proud of implementing that are more difficult to showcase is the talent system and the various player abilities that come with those talents which was mostly my responsibility. Another one is the randomization element of the roguelike dungeon system in the game which was also mostly my responsibility.
